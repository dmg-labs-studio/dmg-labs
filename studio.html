// THE STUDIO

DMG Labs.
Hardware, but fun.

A family-rooted maker studio. The initials are ours — De Mattos Gabriel.

// ORIGIN

An engineer who needed a creative outlet — and found one in the workshop.

I’m an engineer by training, and for a long time I looked at hardware as a problem to be solved. Then something shifted. I started noticing the objects around me — old radios, furniture passed down through families, instruments with brass fittings worn smooth by decades of hands. I wanted to make things like that.

The studio is named for my family. DMG stands for De Mattos Gabriel — the surname my wife and I share across two continents. There’s something of Nuremberg’s precision in how I approach a design, and something of Rio’s warmth in what I want it to feel like when someone holds it.

Everything here comes with full files and guides. If you improve on something I made, I’d genuinely love to know.

// INFLUENCES
Function & Form

Dieter Rams

Good design is as little design as possible. Every device here tries to earn every detail it has — nothing decorative that isn’t also doing something.

Curve & Space

Oskar Niemeyer

The free curve suggests a landscape one hasn’t yet seen. There’s a Brazilian sensibility in how I think about enclosures: no unnecessary right angles.

Light & Contrast

Sebastião Salgado

Deep shadow makes detail visible. That’s how I think about materials — dark wood against warm accents, contrast that lets each element read clearly.

Antique & Ornament

Historic Craft

Antique furniture and instruments show that objects can be both rigorous and richly ornamented. Every DMG device tries to carry something of that into the present.

Three cities.
One studio.

Nuremberg for precision. Rio for warmth. Paris for restraint. We travel a lot and are multinational at core — and that shows up in how we make things. DMG Labs is global by nature, workshop-scale by choice.
