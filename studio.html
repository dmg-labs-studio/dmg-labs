// ORIGIN

An engineer who needed a creative outlet — and found one in the workshop.

I’m an engineer by training. However, I've always desired a more creative outlet in my work. As a passionate musician, playing organ, piano and currently learning horn (not going great), I feel especially attracted to the beautiful worlds that sound can create.

DMG simply stands for the last name created from my wifes and my own birthname: De Mattos Gabriel. My hope is to create things that are fun, beautiful and incorporate core components of our lifes and history. So far, spanning from Paris, Rio and Berlin to Munich.

Everything here (eventually) comes with full files and guides. If you improve on something, I’d genuinely love to know.

// INFLUENCES
Function & Form

Dieter Rams

Good design is as little design as possible. I sometimes try to adhere to that.

Curve & Space

Oskar Niemeyer

The free curve suggests a landscape one hasn’t yet seen. There’s a Brazilian sensibility in how I think about enclosures: no unnecessary right angles.

Light & Contrast

Sebastião Salgado

Deep shadow makes detail visible. Contrast creates vivid experiences.

Antique & Ornament

Historic Craft

Antique furniture and instruments show that objects can be both rigorous and richly ornamented. Every DMG device tries to carry something of that into the present.

Nature

The beauty of life

Nature is the greatest of all artists, having honed beauty and perfection through millions of years of evolution. I love to spend time in nature and observe animals and plants. They shall be my biggest inspiration.

Three cities.
One studio.

Nuremberg for engineering. Rio for sympathy. Paris for judgement. We travel a lot and are multinational at core — and try to incorporate those experiences in the things we make.
