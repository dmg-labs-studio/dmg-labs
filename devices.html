DMG-40

Transit Clock

dmg-40.visual
▶
PLAY VIDEO
1 / 4
FUNCTIONAL ► View Guide

My wife was looking for a real-time departure display of trains and metros she frequently takes from our home in Nuremberg — so I built one for her. Four 8×8 LED matrix panels show the next departing trains and metros in real time, pulling information from the public VGN / VGA APIs. The design is modelled on the elegant roundings of the legendary Braun DN 40 clock, integrating the retro feeling of German train station displays. The red-black color scheme is a nod to the city colors of Nuremberg. The device is self-contained in design and functionality, integrating easily into any stylish office or living space. The ultimate proof of love to urban lifestyle.

specs.txt
Display 4 × 8×8 LED matrix panels
Alternating departure ticker and real-time clock
Connectivity Wi-Fi via Pico 2W Microcontroller
Data Configurable transit API | Custom network and stops possible
(DB, VGN, SNCF, TfL, NYC MTA, ...)
Time NTP-synced real-time clock
Enclosure 3D-printed, inspired by Braun DN40 design language
Custom color schemes available on request
Power USB-C, 5V
Status Fully Operational

DMG-Ph(I)

Acoustic iPhone Dock

dmg-ph(i).visual
▶
PLAY VIDEO
1 / 6
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

Sometimes things can be simple. A docking station for iPhone, inspired by the Braun Phase-1. Charges your phone, while amplifying any audio output by up to +8 dB — the same principle as a gramophone horn, scaled for a phone speaker. The phone inserts horizontally and activates clock, calendar, or picture-frame mode automatically.

specs.txt
Charging MagSafe + USB-C passthrough, 15W
Phone orientation Horizontal, auto-rotates to landscape mode
Amplification Passive acoustic horn
Horn geometry Exponential flare, ~8 dB passive gain
Enclosure 3D-Printed Body, reminiscent of the Braun Phase-I
Compatible iPhone 12 - iPhone 17 Pro (MagSafe)
Not compatible with MAX versions
Status Fully Operational

DMG-LU1

Handheld Music Player

dmg-lu1.visual
▶
PLAY VIDEO
▶ OPEN FULL
1 / 4
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

Remember when you proudly spent hours to create and perfect playlists, to burn them on a CD? That feeling when you finally handed over a masterful assortment of all-time-hits, with some funky design, drawn with colorful sharpies onto the CDs?

The DMG-LU1 let's you feel exactly that.
Physical cassettes with fully customizable designs contain RFID chips that link directly to your playlists — drop one in and the music starts. Stream from Spotify via API, or play local files from SD card. Digitize your old CDs and Vinyls, save them to your Drive and the device automatically pulls the relevant data from the respective source. Perfect for sharing with friends and creating a little "LU1" cassette collection. The 2.13” e-Ink screen is always readable in any light, while channeling a long-forgotten retro vibe.

specs.txt
Processor Raspberry Pi Zero 2W, 512 MB RAM
Display 2.13” e-Ink, bistable (no power when static)
Audio out Bluetooth 5.0 (headphones, speakers, Nautilus dock)
Playback Spotify Connect API or local microSD
RFID PN532 reader — custom cassette inserts trigger playlists
Battery 2000 mAh LiPo, micro-USB charging, ~4 hr playback
Enclosure 3D-printed, fully customizable color combinations
Status Fully operational

DMG-Nautilus

Nautilus Soundbar

dmg-nautilus.visual
1 / 4
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

A high-fidelity soundbar serving as a docking station for the DMG-LU1. The two speaker enclosures follow the golden ratio of the Nautilus spiral to optimize sound quality and minimize standing waves. The Wondom 2x50W bluetooth amp in combination with two Tectonic bending-wave drivers create an impressively full sound stage - no matter where you stand around the system.

specs.txt
Drivers 2 × Tectonic TEBM46C20N-8B Balanced Mode Radiator
Amplifier 2 × 50W Wondom JAB2+ (Stereo)
Target Frequency Response 100 Hz – 20 kHz (±3 dB)
Enclosures Dual nautilus-inspired geometry following the golden ratio for excellent sound quality
Connectivity Bluetooth 5.0, 3.5 mm aux
Status Fully Operational

DMG-Beluga

Beluga Subwoofer

dmg-beluga.visual
1 / 3
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

Matching subwoofer to the DMG-Nautilus. Inspired by the idiosyncratic form of the Beluga whale - one of natures most skilled exploiters of sound waves with its sonar-producing organ. The delicately ornamented back-panel abstracts the cold ocean water of the Beluga's habitat and incorporates design elements from the Nautilus-covers, while elegantly housing the Amp and cabling.
A dual-opposed set-up of the woofers minimizes any mechanical movements of and unwanted sounds from the enclosure while creating a warm, well perceivable bass output.
Connected via bluetooth to the DMG-Nautilus, it creates a unique 2.1 sound system with crystal-clear mids and highs and a warm, present bass from the Beluga.

specs.txt
Drivers 2 × Dayton 115-4
Amplifier Bridged 1x100W Wondom JAB2+ Mono
Target Frequency Range < 120 Hz
Tuning Frequency (Helmholtz Resonator) ~ 48 Hz
Enclosure 3D-Printed Ported Vent Enclosure with Dual-Color Filament
Connectivity Bluetooth 5.0, 3.5 mm aux
2.1 Concert Mode with the DMG-Nautilus
Status Fully Operational
