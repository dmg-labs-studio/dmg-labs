// THE DEVICES

Four devices.
All prototype.

Each one started as a frustration with what was available, and ended as something I’d actually want on my desk. Files and guides are open for all of them.

Electronics
DMG-Ph(1)

Transit Clock

dmg-ph(1).visual
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

A retro-inspired departure board for your home or office. Four 8×8 LED matrix panels show the next departing trains and metros from your city in real time, pulling from public transit APIs. The display is modelled on the warm amber glow of Braun Phase 1 clock aesthetics — functional, legible, and worth looking at.

specs.txt
Display 4 × MAX7219 8×8 LED matrix panels, warm amber
Connectivity Wi-Fi via ESP32 microcontroller
Data Configurable transit API (Deutsche Bahn, VGN, SNCF, TfL, NYC MTA)
Time NTP-synced real-time clock, displayed on left two panels
Enclosure FDM-printed, inspired by Braun Phase 1 proportions
Power USB-C, 5V
Status Prototype — firmware functional, enclosure v1 printed

DMG-40

Acoustic iPhone Dock

dmg-40.visual
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

A docking station for iPhone that doubles as a passive acoustic amplifier. The phone inserts horizontally and activates clock, calendar, or picture-frame mode automatically. Below, a purely mechanical trumpet-horn carved from hardwood increases audio volume without any electronics — the same principle as a gramophone horn, scaled for a phone speaker.

specs.txt
Charging MagSafe + USB-C passthrough, 15W
Phone orientation Horizontal, auto-rotates to landscape mode
Amplification Passive acoustic horn, hardwood (walnut or oak)
Horn geometry Exponential flare, ~8 dB passive gain
Enclosure FDM body + CNC-routed or turned wood horn
Compatible iPhone 12 and later (MagSafe ring)
Status Prototype — mechanical horn v1 complete, dock electronics in progress
Electronics
DMG-LU1

Handheld Music Player

dmg-lu1.visual
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

A handheld music player that brings ownership and haptics back to digital listening. Stream from Spotify via API, or play local files from SD card. Physical cassettes contain RFID chips that link directly to playlists — drop one in and the music starts. The 2.13” e-Ink screen is always readable in any light, and the Raspberry Pi Zero 2W provides full Bluetooth audio output.

specs.txt
Processor Raspberry Pi Zero 2W, 512 MB RAM
Display 2.13” e-Ink, 250×122 px, bistable (no power when static)
Audio out Bluetooth 5.0 (headphones, speakers, Nautilus dock)
Playback Spotify Connect API + local FLAC/MP3 from microSD
RFID PN532 reader — custom cassette inserts trigger playlists
Battery 2000 mAh LiPo, USB-C charging, ~8 hr playback
Enclosure FDM-printed, Niemeyer-curved edges, replaceable face panels
Status Prototype — hardware assembled, firmware in development
Audio
DMG-Nautilus

Nautilus Soundbar

dmg-nautilus.visual
PROTOTYPE ► View Guide

A high-fidelity soundbar designed around two Tectonic TEBM46C20N-4B bending-wave drivers — a technology that produces a remarkably uniform frequency response across a wide listening area. The two speaker enclosures follow the golden ratio nautilus spiral geometry to optimise internal volume and minimise standing waves. The soundbar also serves as a docking station for the DMG-LU1.

specs.txt
Drivers 2 × Tectonic TEBM46C20N-4B Balanced Mode Radiator
Amplifier 2 × 50W Class-D (TPA3116), 100W total
Frequency response 100 Hz – 20 kHz (±3 dB)
Enclosures Dual nautilus-spiral geometry, golden ratio proportions, FDM + resin
LU1 Dock Integrated pogo-pin dock, charges and streams via Bluetooth
Connectivity Bluetooth 5.0, optical in, 3.5 mm aux
Enclosure FDM structural shell, wood veneer panels
Status Prototype — enclosure design complete, amplifier board in progress
